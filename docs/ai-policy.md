# AI Policy

## Purpose

Defines how artificial-intelligence and machine-learning systems are
developed, deployed, and operated at the organisation. Aligns with
ISO/IEC 42001 Annex A.2 (Policies related to AI) and ISO/IEC 23894
(AI risk management).

## Scope

All AI and ML systems developed, procured, deployed, or operated by the
organisation — including third-party AI services embedded in our products.

## Policy Principles

1. **Human oversight** — a human is accountable for every automated
   decision that materially affects a customer.
2. **Transparency** — users are informed when they are interacting with
   an AI system and what data it uses.
3. **Fairness** — models are evaluated for bias across protected
   attributes before release.
4. **Safety and robustness** — models are stress-tested for adversarial
   inputs and known failure modes.
5. **Data governance** — training data is sourced, labelled, and
   retained in accordance with the Data Protection Policy.

## Policy Review

This policy is reviewed at least annually by the AI Governance Committee
(see AI Governance Committee Charter). Out-of-cycle reviews are triggered
by: a material change in AI capability, a new regulatory requirement, or
a serious incident involving an AI system.

## Communication

Approved versions of this policy are distributed to all staff via the
employee handbook and published on the internal compliance portal.
